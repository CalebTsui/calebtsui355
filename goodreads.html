Connecting Avid Readers Through a Redesigned Goodreads Community app flow and interface

In this 4-week academic project, I was tasked to investigate an existing mobile app, Goodreads, to identify problems through research and redesign a specific features to troubleshoot and improve the overall experience.

* This case study is not affiliated with Goodreads as a client and this project was used for educational purposes.

My Role

UX/UI Design, UX Research

Team

Individual

Duration

3-week (Spring 2024)

Background

So...Who is Goodreads?

Goodreads serves as a social cataloging platform, providing book enthusiast with a space to discover new books, express their views through reviews and ratings, and build connections with fellow readers who share similar literary interests.

Some of the Key Application Features

Social interactions between users

Users can join online book clubs to engage with others. Also, readers can share their opinions by leaving reviews and ratings for books.

Discover new books based on user activities

Based on the user‚Äôs in-app activity, the mobile app‚Äôs algorithm can create lists of recommended books that may interest the readers.

Virtual bookshelves to organize progress

Users can create and customize virtual bookshelves to organize and categorize their reading lists, while also tracking their reading progress.

Missions & Goals

Connecting bookworms around the world.

Assisting readers in discovering more books

Organize their books and keep track of their reading progress.
Research

Speaking with Goodreads‚Äô users with the mobile app to understand the usability issues, pain points, and potential design opportunities.

I began the research by conducting a heuristic walkthrough of the app, documenting usability issues and observations. These findings then informed the planning of user interviews, which were used to validate and expand on the identified insights.

Within two days, I recruited and interviewed 4 participants across two rounds: an initial exploratory interview followed by a more targeted session focused on specific features (Community, Bookshelves, & Discovery). Participants were guided through structured questions and task-based activities while their interactions with the mobile app were observed.

The two-round interviews revealed these following main pain points:

Pain Point #1

the UI and navigation flows are outdated and confusing, discouraging them from other features.

Pain Point #2

some features are hidden or very rough so discourage them from retention

Pain Point #3

Some features do not translate from the desktop version to the mobile version.

When asked about the Community, Bookshelves & Discovery Features...

Pain Point #1

Most interviewees find the community feature lacklustre compared to the other two, as it appears as just a blank page with a search bar.

Pain Point #2

The current Community Page fails to motivate or engage users because it does not offer meaningful value with limited interaction and confusing flow.

Also note, one interviewee did not even know the community feature existed until the interview took place as they quote, "it's way too hidden in the app..."

The Problem

The Community feature lacks a clear perceived value for readers, leading to minimal engagement. Its limited interactions undermine the platform‚Äôs goal of building a connected reading community.

But is it actually a big deal though? Well...Based on the interviews and online forums:

Community Drives Discovery

Readers value communities because they help surface new books and offer diverse interpretations that enrich the reading experience.

Shared Goals Create Motivation

Whether in small or large groups, reading alongside others fosters motivation, accountability, and a sense of shared progress.

Connection Matters Beyond Habit

Even though avid readers would read regardless, communities add emotional value by creating opportunities to bond over shared interests.

Belonging Drives Engagement

Users are drawn to features that make them feel part of something larger, reinforcing long-term engagement and platform loyalty.

Design Focus

How might we redesign the Community feature to feel more meaningful and increase perceived value and engagement, supporting Goodreads‚Äô mission of fostering a connected reading community?

Amplify User‚Äôs Motivation Through Clear Value

The goal isn‚Äôt to force reading or create a community for its own sake. It‚Äôs to improve existing users‚Äô motivation by clearly demonstrating the value of participating and increasing¬†engagement depth.

Intended Audience

Based on research insights, two user personas were developed to represent distinct reading habits, motivations, and levels of community engagement. These personas guided the design decisions and helped ensure the solution addressed real user needs and behaviours.

The Social Reader

A habitual reader who enjoys discovering new books and connecting with others about shared reading experiences.

Goals + Motivations

  • Connect with like-minded readers and share interpretations of books.
  • Discover new books and genres through social interactions.
  • Feel a sense of belonging and engagement in the reading community.

The Casual Bookworm

An occasional reader who wants to be part of a small reading group and accountability to maintain a consistent reading habit.

Goals + Motivations

  • Participate in reading activities and be included in a community without feeling forced.
  • Reinforce reading habits through social accountability.
  • Read other people‚Äôs reviews and discussions
Proposed Solution

Below are the refinements proposed for the Community feature. The designs were developed based on user research insights and Goodreads‚Äô existing Branding and UI patterns to minimize the learning curve for users and reduce redevelopment effort.

Refining Community Discovery to Improve Group Finding and Joining

The existing Community home and discovery pages provide limited guidance on what users should do next. The refined design improves content structure and navigation to support intuitive decision-making, reducing the need for users to guess interactions and flows, as highlighted in user interviews.

Improving Group Detail Pages for Clearer Actions and User Engagement

The existing Community group detail page has unclear UI design and call-to-action buttons, resulting in minimal engagement both with the content and between users. The refined proposal restructures the layout and introduces clearer actions and interactive elements to support richer interaction opportunities.

Bringing Group Creation to the Mobile Experience

At present, Group creation is restricted to desktops. The proposed solution brings these capabilities to mobile, enabling a more accessible and simplified group creation experience.

Usability Testing

The prototype was evaluated with three participants, and the insights gathered were used to refine key features.

The test focused on assessing the usability of the proposed community features and how effectively users could complete primary tasks, including finding a new group to join and creating a new group. The Participants were asked to complete realistic scenarios using the Think Aloud method to surface any usability issues.

Below are the major fixes made to the initial prototype:

Update: The ‚ÄúCreate Group‚Äù button was repositioned to the top of the page to reduce interference with content.

Update: The Drop-down Tabs

Problem: Participants expected a drop-down based on the arrow icon for each chat room and felt it would be confusing to place all topics in a single chat room.

Update: To address user feedback, the chat was restructured into a drop-down, allowing different topics to have their own chat rooms.

Conclusion

From this experience, I learned the importance of adaptability and proactive decision-making. By the second week, I realized that I was falling behind and that my process was not working. Instead of prolonging the issue, I decided to restart the project and adjust my approach to stay on track and ensure a strong process. This lesson reinforced the importance of reflecting on my process, recognizing what isn‚Äôt working, and taking timely action to improve outcomes in design projects.
